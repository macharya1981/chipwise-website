India's Strategic Push into Semiconductor Manufacturing

Published: Aug 10, 2025

The landscape of semiconductor manufacturing in India is evolving at a rapid pace, driven by a national vision to become a key player in the global technology ecosystem. This strategic push is not just about building factories; it's about creating a robust, self-sufficient supply chain and fostering a new generation of skilled talent.

A Growing Market and Major Investments

On India's 79th Independence Day, Prime Minister Narendra Modi's announcement of six new chip units marked a significant milestone. Four of these projects, representing a substantial investment of **₹4,600 crore**, have already been approved. This commitment is a direct response to a burgeoning domestic market, which is projected to reach an impressive **US$109 billion by 2030**.

These newly sanctioned projects are strategically located to build a diverse manufacturing base:

  • Odisha: A state-of-the-art compound semiconductor fabrication facility is being established by SiCSem Private Limited, in partnership with UK-based Clas-SiC Wafer Fab Ltd. This will be India’s first commercial Silicon Carbide (SiC) facility.
  • Punjab: An advanced glass substrate packaging unit is set to boost the country’s backend manufacturing capabilities.
  • Andhra Pradesh: A new high-power discrete component manufacturing facility will help meet the specific needs of various industries.

The Strategic Importance of Domestic Chip Production

The government's support for these facilities is designed to achieve several critical objectives:

  • **Enhance Domestic Capacity:** The new units will significantly increase India's chip design and manufacturing capabilities, fostering a stronger local industry.
  • **Reduce Import Dependency:** By producing more chips domestically, India can reduce its reliance on foreign imports and secure its technology supply chain.
  • **Meet Surging Demand:** The new facilities are expected to cater to the growing demand from vital sectors such as telecom, automotive, defense, and consumer electronics.
  • **Generate Skilled Jobs:** These projects are projected to create over **2,000 direct skilled jobs** and a large number of indirect roles in the wider electronics supply chain.

This proactive approach signals a new era for India's technology sector. The success of these ventures hinges on a well-trained workforce, a challenge that ChipWise is uniquely positioned to address by bridging the skill gap and preparing the next generation of semiconductor professionals.
